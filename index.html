Childhood

Martin Luther King Jr. was born on January 15, 1929 in Atlanta, Georgia. he had three siblings, A.D. King, Christine King Farris, and Donn Clendenon who played for the Mets in '71 and for the Cardinals in '72. As a child Martin wanted to be a fireman, not knowing that he would actually become one of the most famous civil rights activists in the world. He enjoyed playing sports like baseball, football, and even got piano lessons from his mom.

Education

Young Martin started his elementary education at Yonge Street Elementary in Atlanta but, when it was discovered that he was not the school required age of six his education was halted. Once he reached the age of six he continued Kindergarten at David T. Howard Elementary. In high school he skipped both ninth and twelth grade causing to go to Morehouse College at just fifteen years old, and in 1948 he graduated at the age of nineteen with a B.A. degree in Sociology.

"I have a dream"

Dr. Martin Luther King gave his "I Have a Dream" speech on August 28, 1963 during the march on washington. As he stood in front of The Lincoln Memorial giving his speech 250,000 people were there listening to him with even more listening on the radio and television. This caused the Civil Rights Movement to take a huge upswing and Martin Luther King was now world famous, his speech had helped pass the Civil Rights Act of 1964, and he had provided momentum for the Voting Rights Act.
